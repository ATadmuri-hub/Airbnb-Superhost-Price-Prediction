Airbnb Superhost & Price Prediction

21 supervised learning models applied to 50,000+ Airbnb listings across Madrid, Barcelona, and Amsterdam. Classification, regression, SHAP interpretability, and beyond-course methods.

Key Findings

  • Superhost status is a behaviour story — host responsiveness, acceptance rate, and tenure are the strongest predictors
  • Pricing is a property story — listing capacity, location, and neighbourhood context dominate price prediction
  • Random Forest achieves the highest classification AUC; XGBoost leads regression
  • Calibration matters — linear models produce better-calibrated probabilities despite lower AUC

Results

Listing locations coloured by nightly price across Amsterdam, Barcelona, and Madrid. Price hotspots concentrate near city centres and tourist districts.
Superhost geographic distribution. Unlike price, Superhosts are distributed citywide — behaviour matters more than location.
SHAP beeswarm plot for XGBoost classification. Each dot is one observation. Host total listings count, estimated occupancy, and response rate are the top drivers.
Calibration curves for all 12 classification models. Points on the diagonal indicate perfect calibration. Linear models (GLM, Elastic Net) are best calibrated despite lower AUC.
SHAP dependence plots for the top 3 classification predictors, revealing non-linear relationships and interaction effects.
Spearman correlation of predicted probabilities across all classification models. High correlation between GLM/ENet/RF suggests ensemble diversity is limited in those pairs.
